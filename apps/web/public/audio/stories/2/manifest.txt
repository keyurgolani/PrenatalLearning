Long ago, about 350 years before you were born, a young man named Isaac Newton sat in a garden, thinking about the world. Legend says that an apple fell from a tree and bonked him on the head. Whether or not that's exactly true, Newton began to wonder: why do things fall down?! It seems like such a simple question, little one. But Newton realized something extraordinary. The same invisible pull that made the apple fall was also keeping the Moon circling around the Earth, and the Earth circling around the Sun!! Newton called this invisible pull "gravity." He discovered that everything in the universe; every star, every planet, every apple, every grain of sand; pulls on everything else. The bigger something is, the stronger its pull. That's why the Earth, which is very, very big, pulls you toward it so strongly.! Imagine, little one, that everything in the universe is connected by invisible strings of attraction. The Sun pulls on the Earth, the Earth pulls on the Moon, and the Earth pulls on you. These invisible strings hold the whole universe together in a beautiful cosmic dance.! The Trampoline of Space: Einstein's Amazing Idea! For over 200 years, everyone thought Newton had figured out gravity completely. But then came a man named Albert Einstein, with wild hair and even wilder ideas.! Einstein asked a different question: How does gravity actually work? How does the Earth "know" to pull on the Moon? How does the Sun reach out across millions of miles of empty space to hold the planets in their orbits?! Einstein's answer was so strange and beautiful that it changed how we understand the entire universe.! Imagine, little one, a big trampoline stretched out flat. Now imagine placing a heavy bowling ball in the middle. What happens? The trampoline bends and curves around the bowling ball, creating a dip.! Now roll a marble across the trampoline. Does it go in a straight line? No!

And when other objects move through this bent space, they follow the curves, which looks to us like they're being pulled by gravity.! But here's the truly magical part: it's not just space that bends. Time bends too! Near heavy objects, time actually moves more slowly. Einstein called this bendy, stretchy thing "spacetime"; because space and time are woven together like threads in a fabric. If the sheet is flat, you walk in a straight line. But if someone puts a heavy ball on the sheet, creating a dip, your "straight line" becomes a curve around the dip. You're not being pulled by the ball; you're just following the shape of the sheet.! That's what planets do! The Earth isn't being pulled toward the Sun by an invisible rope. Instead, the Sun bends spacetime around it, and the Earth follows the curves in that bent spacetime. It's like the Earth is rolling around the inside of a giant cosmic bowl created by the Sun.! Falling Without Falling: The Secret of Orbits! Here's something wonderful to think about, little one. The Moon is falling toward the Earth right now. Yes, falling! But it never hits us. How can that be?! Imagine throwing a ball. It curves down and hits the ground, right? Now imagine throwing it harder; it goes farther before it lands.

# coreContent|4|coreContent-part4.mp3|That's what a black hole is like. It's a place where something incredibly massive has been squeezed into a tiny space, bending spacetime so much that nothing; not even light; can escape.! Black holes are like cosmic vacuum cleaners, but they don't suck things in from far away. You'd have to get very close to fall in. From far away, a black hole's gravity is just like any other object's. But cross that invisible boundary called the event horizon, and there's no coming back.! Near a black hole, time slows down dramatically. If you could watch someone falling into a black hole from far away, you'd see them slow down and freeze at the edge, their image getting redder and dimmer until they fade from view. Two black holes, each about 30 times heavier than our Sun, had spiraled together and merged, sending ripples through the fabric of spacetime that traveled across the universe for over a billion years before reaching Earth.! These ripples stretched and squeezed space itself by an amount smaller than the width of a single atom. But scientists built machines sensitive enough to feel them. It was like hearing the universe whisper its secrets for the very first time.! Gravity Holds Us Together! And so, little one, gravity is the gentle giant of the universe. It holds galaxies together, keeps planets in their orbits, makes stars shine, and keeps your feet on the ground.! Without gravity, there would be no stars to cook the elements that make up your body. Without gravity, there would be no Earth to call home. Without gravity, you wouldn't be cradled so safely in your mother's womb.!
