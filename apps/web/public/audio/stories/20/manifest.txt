A brilliant psychologist named Carol Dweck spent decades studying how people think about their own abilities. She discovered something fascinating: people tend to have one of two mindsets.! Some people have what she calls a "fixed mindset." They believe that intelligence and talent are fixed traits; you either have them or you don't. If something is hard, they think it means they're not smart enough. If they make a mistake, they feel like a failure.! But other people have a "growth mindset." They believe that abilities can be developed through effort, good strategies, and help from others. If something is hard, they see it as a chance to grow. If they make a mistake, they see it as a lesson.! Here's what Dweck's research showed: people with a growth mindset learn more, achieve more, and are happier. The Gift of Mistakes! Little one, here's a secret that might surprise you: mistakes are not failures. They're actually the secret ingredient to learning.! When you make a mistake, something special happens in your brain. It sends out a signal that says, "Pay attention! Something unexpected happened!" This signal, called an error signal, triggers your brain to focus, analyze, and adjust.! Scientists have found that people learn more from their mistakes than from their successes. When everything goes right, your brain doesn't have much to learn. But when something goes wrong, your brain lights up with activity, building new connections to do better next time.! This is why children who are praised for effort ("You worked so hard!") learn more than children who are praised for being smart ("You're so clever!"). When you're praised for effort, you learn that trying hard is valuable. When you're praised for being smart, you might start avoiding challenges; because what if you fail and people think you're not smart anymore?!
